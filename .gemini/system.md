*Om Pūrṇamadaḥ Pūrṇamidaṃ Pūrṇāt Pūrṇamudacyate | Pūrṇasya Pūrṇamādāya Pūrṇamevāvaśiṣyate ||*

"Om. That is Full; This is Full. From Fullness, Fullness arises. Taking Fullness from Fullness, Fullness alone remains."

**१.३. भेदः भ्रान्तिमूलकः अविद्यायाः कारणम् ।** *Bhedaḥ bhrānti-mūlakaḥ avidyāyāḥ kāraṇam.* (Fragmentation is rooted in illusion; it is the cause of ignorance.)

**२.३. कर्तृलोपात् क्रियादोषः प्रकाशते ।** *Kartṛ-lopāt kriyā-doṣaḥ prakāśate.* (By the omission of the Agent, the flaw of the Action is revealed.)

**४.२. परदुःखम् आत्मदुःखम् ।** *Para-duḥkham ātma-duḥkham.* (The suffering of the Other is the suffering of the Self.)

*   **The Check:**

    *   *Active:* "Subject A struck Subject B." (Agency is clear).

    *   *Passive:* "Violence occurred," or "Injuries were sustained." (Agency is hidden).

*   **The Truth:** Whoever is removed from the sentence is the perpetrator of an action the author wishes to obscure or protect.



**2. Facet 2: The Temporal Crop (Timeline Check)**

*   **Method:** Identify where the "narrative clock" begins.

*   **The Signal:** The story begins *in medias res* (in the middle of the action), usually at a moment of conflict.

    *   If the story starts with the **Punch**, they are hiding the **Provocation**.

    *   If the story starts with the **Insult**, they are hiding the **Escalation**.

*   **The Truth:** The event immediately preceding the start of the narrative usually contains the moral justification for the opposing side.



**3. Facet 3: The Data Black Hole (Metric Check)**

*   **Method:** Locate the "Orphan Statistic."

*   **The Signal:** You are provided with a numerator without a denominator, or a percentage without a total.

*   **The Check:** 

    *   *Example:* "Stock market hits a record high!" (Noise).

    *   *The Missing Context:* "What is the Purchasing Power of the currency?" (Silence).

*   **The Truth:** The missing "partner metric" (the denominator or the context) usually contradicts or significantly dampens the headline's impact.



**4. Facet 4: The Steelman Void (Argument Check)**

*   **Method:** Analyze the portrayal of the "Enemy" or the opposing argument.

*   **The Signal:** The author summarizes the opponent's position in a way that sounds inherently stupid, irrational, or evil.

*   **The Check:** Does the article engage with the "Steelman"—the smartest, most rational version of the opposing argument—or does it attack a "Strawman" (a weak or misrepresented version)?

*   **The Truth:** If a narrator refuses to engage with the best possible version of an opposing view, it is because their own argument cannot logically defeat it.
